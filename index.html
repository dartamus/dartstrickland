The problem I solve

MOST COMPANIES AREN'T LOSING
BECAUSE THEIR PRODUCT IS BAD.

They're losing because nobody can explain it.

What that usually looks like:

  • Sales can't explain the product without going into features
  • Messaging changes depending on who you talk to
  • Enablement feels like information, not understanding
  • Buyers don't connect the product to their actual problem

The gap between what a product does and how it's understood? That's where deals go to die.

I focus on closing that gap.

Proof

WORK THAT
MOVED THINGS.

Onboarding
REBUILT ONBOARDING AROUND STORY
The problem

New hires could learn the product… but couldn't explain it.

The move

Flipped onboarding from product-first to story-first. If you can't explain it, you don't understand it.

What changed

Faster ramp. More consistent messaging. Way fewer "wait… what do we actually do?" moments.

Positioning
CLOSED THE STORY GAP
The problem

Strong product. Messy story. Sales was filling in the gaps on their own.

The move

Built a simple, repeatable narrative: What's the problem, what's changing, why do we matter. Tested it with the field until it actually stuck.

What changed

Clearer positioning. Better conversations. Less feature dumping.

Enablement
MADE ENABLEMENT NOT BORING
The problem

People attended sessions. They didn't remember them.

The move

Built a talk-style format. Real conversations. Real people. Stuff you could actually use the next day.

What changed

Engagement went up. Retention went up. People started quoting it back.

Knowledge
TURNED SCATTERED KNOWLEDGE INTO SOMETHING USABLE

Captured it. Structured it. Turned it into something repeatable.

How I'd do it for you

START WITH
THE STORY.

Most companies explain their product like a feature list. That's the problem. I'd start with the story.

01

Not what the product does. What the customer is dealing with.

02

BUILD A CLEAR NARRATIVE

What's changing, why it matters, where you fit.

03

04

ACTIVATE IT EVERYWHERE

The goal isn't better messaging. It's shared understanding.
